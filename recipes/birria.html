Birria de Res

Description

Birria de Res is a flavorful and aromatic Mexican stew traditionally made with beef. This dish, originating from Jalisco, is known for its rich broth, tender meat, and the deep, complex flavors imparted by a combination of dried chiles and spices. The beef is marinated and then slow-cooked until it falls apart easily, resulting in a deliciously savory stew. Birria de Res can be served as a soup with the broth or used as a filling for tacos, garnished with fresh cilantro, chopped onions, and a squeeze of lime for an extra burst of flavor.

This dish is often enjoyed during special occasions and family gatherings, bringing people together over its hearty and comforting taste. The process of making Birria de Res involves marinating the beef in a vibrant chile sauce, then simmering it slowly to allow the flavors to meld perfectly. The result is a mouthwatering stew that captures the essence of traditional Mexican cuisine, offering a delightful combination of spice, warmth, and robust taste.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs. beef chuck roast, cut into large chunks
  • 8 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 4 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups water
  • Corn tortillas for serving
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade: Blend guajillo and ancho chiles with onion, garlic, cumin, oregano, black pepper, salt, and vinegar until smooth.
  2. Marinate the Meat: Coat the beef with the chile mixture and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Cook the Meat: In a large pot, add the marinated beef, chicken broth, and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 3-4 hours until the meat is tender.
  4. Serve: Serve the birria with warm tortillas, garnished with fresh cilantro and lime wedges.
